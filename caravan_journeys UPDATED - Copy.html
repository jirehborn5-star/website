Our Destinations

Iran

A cultural treasure of ancient civilizations, stunning Islamic architecture, and legendary hospitality. Explore historic cities, magnificent palaces, and breathtaking natural landscapes in one of the world's oldest continuously inhabited regions.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) offer pleasant weather. Summer can be hot; winter brings snow in mountainous areas.

Visa Requirements

Most nationalities need a visa. We assist with invitation letters and forms.

Safety Information

Iran is generally safe with low crime rates. Our guides help you navigate cultural norms; we track local conditions.

Local Currency & Payment

The local currency is the Iranian Rial. Credit cards are rarely accepted, so bring cash. We'll advise on current exchange rates and money handling.

Language

Farsi (Persian) is the official language. English is spoken in tourist areas and by our guides. We provide a basic language guide with essential phrases.

Internet & Communications

WiFi is available in most hotels and some public areas. Local SIM cards can be purchased. Some websites and apps are restricted, so consider a VPN.

What to Pack

Pack modest clothing that covers shoulders, arms, and legs. Women should bring a headscarf. Comfortable walking shoes and layers for varied temperatures are essential.

Health Considerations

No special vaccinations are required. Bottled water is recommended. We provide a comprehensive pre-departure health guide and carry first aid kits.

Afghanistan

A crossroads of civilizations with breathtaking mountain landscapes and remarkable cultural heritage. Our carefully selected journeys focus on architectural treasures, stunning natural beauty, and authentic hospitality in safe, accessible regions.

Best Time to Visit

April-June & September-October are ideal. High summer can be hot; winters may bring heavy snow in mountain areas.

Visa Requirements

All visitors require a visa. We provide invitation letters, etc. Apply 8+ weeks before travel.

Safety Information

We only operate where local experts deem secure, with risk-assessed itineraries and experienced guides.

Local Currency & Payment

The local currency is the Afghani. Credit cards are rarely accepted, so bring cash. We'll advise on current exchange rates and money handling.

Language

Dari (Afghan Persian) and Pashto are the official languages. English is spoken by our guides. We provide a basic language guide with essential phrases.

Internet & Communications

WiFi is available in most hotels but can be unreliable. Local SIM cards provide better connectivity in cities. We provide emergency satellite communication.

What to Pack

Pack modest clothing that covers shoulders, arms, and legs. Women should bring a headscarf. Comfortable walking shoes and layers for varied temperatures are essential.

Health Considerations

Check with your travel doctor for recommended vaccinations. Bottled water is essential. We provide a comprehensive pre-departure health guide and carry medical kits.

Lebanon

A compact Mediterranean gem offering vibrant Beirut, ancient Phoenician cities, Roman ruins, and beautiful coastlines. Our upcoming journeys will explore this captivating blend of cultures and landscapes.

Best Time to Visit

Spring & autumn have pleasant temps. Summers warm on the coast, cooler in mountains; winter can bring snow at higher elevations.

Visa Requirements

Many nationalities can obtain a visa on arrival; regulations vary. We'll assist with the latest details when finalizing your trip.

Safety Information

We continuously assess local conditions. Our tours focus on stable areas, working closely with local experts for safety.

Local Currency & Payment

The local currency is the Lebanese Pound. US dollars are widely accepted. ATMs are available in cities but may have withdrawal limits. We'll provide current currency advice.

Language

Arabic is the official language, but French and English are widely spoken, especially in tourist areas. Our guides are multilingual to assist with communication.

Internet & Communications

WiFi is available in most hotels and cafes. Local SIM cards can be purchased for mobile data. Power outages may occur, so a portable charger is recommended.

What to Pack

Lebanon is relatively liberal, but modest clothing is recommended for religious sites. Pack layers for varied temperatures, comfortable walking shoes, and beachwear if visiting coastal areas.

Health Considerations

No special vaccinations are required. Bottled water is recommended. Medical facilities are good in Beirut. We provide first aid kits and can arrange access to quality healthcare if needed.

Persian Cultural Heritage

Immerse yourself in Iran's rich cultural heritage on this 7-day journey through the country's most significant historical sites.

Itinerary Highlights

Day 1: Tehran

Begin your journey in Iran's capital. Visit the UNESCO-listed Golestan Palace complex, explore the National Museum of Iran to see artifacts dating back to the 5th millennium BCE, and enjoy the contemporary art at the Tehran Museum of Contemporary Art.

Day 2-3: Isfahan

Travel to Isfahan, known for its Persian-Islamic architecture. Explore the UNESCO-listed Naqsh-e Jahan Square, Shah Mosque, Sheikh Lotfollah Mosque, and the Ali Qapu Palace. Stroll along the historic bridges spanning the Zayandeh River at sunset.

Day 4-5: Shiraz

Continue to Shiraz, the city of poets and gardens. Visit the Pink Mosque (Nasir al-Mulk), known for its stunning stained glass and intricate tilework. Explore the tombs of famous Persian poets Hafez and Saadi, and stroll through the beautiful Eram Garden.

Day 6: Persepolis

Visit the ancient ceremonial capital of the Achaemenid Empire. Explore the grand staircases, imposing gateways, and detailed reliefs depicting nations of the Achaemenid Empire. Continue to nearby Necropolis (Naqsh-e Rostam) to view ancient royal tombs.

Day 7: Tehran

Return to Tehran for your final day. Visit the Treasury of National Jewels to see the dazzling collection of the Persian monarchy, explore the historic Grand Bazaar, and enjoy a farewell dinner featuring traditional Persian cuisine before departure.

Natural Wonders of Iran

Discover Iran's diverse landscapes on this 6-day adventure from mountains to deserts to lush forests.

Itinerary Highlights

Day 1: Tehran & Alborz Mountains

Begin your journey in Tehran. After a brief city orientation, head to the magnificent Alborz mountain range. Enjoy a scenic drive to Mount Tochal and take the telecabin for stunning views of the city and mountains. Stay overnight in a mountain resort.

Day 2: Hyrcanian Forests

Travel north through the mountains to the UNESCO-listed Hyrcanian Forests along the Caspian Sea. Explore ancient deciduous forest that dates back to the Tertiary Period, discovering unique flora and fauna found nowhere else in the world. Overnight in a forest lodge.

Day 3: Caspian Coast & Return to Tehran

Continue exploring the lush Caspian region, visiting traditional villages and local markets. Enjoy the contrast between mountain forests and coastal landscapes. In the afternoon, return to Tehran for an overnight stay before your desert journey.

Day 4: Kerman & Lut Desert

Embark on a scenic drive from Tehran to Kerman through diverse landscapes. The journey offers views of mountains, desert plains, and traditional villages. Continue to the edge of the UNESCO-listed Lut Desert and arrive at your desert camp for sunset and stargazing.

Day 5: Lut Desert Exploration

Full day exploring the remarkable geological formations of the Lut Desert, including the yardangs (kaluts) and massive sand dunes. Experience the otherworldly landscape of one of the hottest places on Earth. Visit the ancient city of Kerman in the late afternoon.

Day 6: Tehran

Morning exploration of Kerman city with its historic bazaar and traditional architecture. Begin the return drive to Tehran, stopping at interesting sites along the way. Arrive in Tehran for a farewell dinner featuring dishes from the various regions visited during your journey.

Historical Afghanistan

Journey through Afghanistan's rich cultural history on this 8-day expedition to significant historical sites.

Itinerary Highlights

Day 1: Kabul Arrival

Begin your journey in Afghanistan's capital. Visit the National Museum to understand the country's archaeological heritage and explore the historic Gardens of Babur. Meet with local historians and cultural experts for a welcome dinner.

Day 2-3: Bamiyan Valley

Fly to the Bamiyan Valley, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Explore the niches of the Giant Buddhas, visit the nearby caves with ancient paintings, and trek to the turquoise Band-e Amir lakes. Interact with local Hazara communities and learn about their traditions.

Day 4-5: Herat

Fly to the ancient city of Herat near the Iranian border. Visit the magnificent Friday Mosque (Masjid-i Jami), explore the restored ancient citadel, and wander through the old city with its traditional bazaars. Meet with local artisans practicing traditional crafts.

Day 6-7: Mazar-i-Sharif & Balkh

Fly to Mazar-i-Sharif. Visit the stunning Blue Mosque (Shrine of Hazrat Ali) with its distinctive architecture and blue tiles. Explore the ancient Greco-Bactrian city of Balkh, known as the "Mother of Cities" and once home to Zoroaster.

Day 8: Return to Kabul

Fly back to Kabul. Visit Bibi Mahru Hill for panoramic views of the city, explore the vibrant Chicken Street market for traditional crafts and antiques, and enjoy a farewell dinner featuring dishes from the various regions visited during your journey.

Silk Road Expedition

Follow the footsteps of ancient traders on this 5-day expedition along Afghanistan's section of the historic Silk Road.

Itinerary Highlights

Day 1: Kabul

Begin in Kabul with a visit to the National Museum to understand Afghanistan's role in Silk Road trade. Explore Murad Khani, the historic commercial district, and meet with scholars specializing in Afghanistan's trade history.

Day 2: Bamiyan

Fly to Bamiyan, once a major Buddhist center and important Silk Road stop. Explore the Buddha niches and nearby caves where merchants would rest and pray. Visit Shahr-e Gholghola (the City of Screams), a fortress city that was a critical Silk Road trading post.

Day 3: Herat

Fly to Herat, one of the most important Silk Road cities in the region. Explore the restored caravanserais, the old covered bazaar, and the centuries-old workshops where traditional crafts from the Silk Road era are still practiced.

Day 4: Balkh

Fly to Mazar-i-Sharif and visit ancient Balkh, once known as Bactra, where merchants from China, India, and the Mediterranean would meet. Visit the remains of ancient walls, stupas, and explore the region's role as a center of Buddhist, Zoroastrian, and Islamic learning.

Day 5: Return to Kabul

Fly back to Kabul. Visit traditional craft markets to see items similar to those traded on the ancient Silk Road. Explore the artifacts in the Kabul Museum that were found along the Silk Road routes, and enjoy a farewell dinner featuring dishes inspired by historical Silk Road cuisine.
